It's greed, plain and simple

There’s a reason it's one of the seven deadly sins.

It warps a mind and separates a person from their fellow citizens. It feeds corruption and pushes standards lower in the pursuit of profit and a constant justification that all rewards are deserved and properly earned. It’s self-feeding, loatheful, and shamed in every religion yet our culture has grown to love it.  

Our shows and movies glorify a constant pursuit of material goods and experiences at the cost of our fellow humans and coworkers. Competition forces us apart when there’s never been a bigger need for us all to work together.  We – the wage-laborers of the United States, the people who simply want to make a decent enough living to provide for our families without relying on debt, the people who keep this country running every day safely and consistently, the people who fight our fires and keep our streets safe, any person in this great nation that takes pride in their work and is forced to rely on someone else to sign their paycheck.

So what can we do?

On a personal level, work towards being more content with fewer material things and spending more time with your family and friends doing things you love to do. Volunteer your time and donate posessions and money to charities you support. Honestly realize and believe that you can change things and try to avoid cynicism.

At your workplace, start spreading the word while avoiding direct confrontation. Ask for a raise with the knowledge that the payroll budget is only being eaten up by the executive’s salary. Tell your coworkers to do the same. Calmly and individually bring up higher wages to your HR director and ask probing questions about executive pay. Why do we make so much less? Is it justified? Perhaps you’ll even get the HR director thinking.  

In your community – come up with materials similar to this site. Flyers, handouts, ads, billboards, anything to call attention to the vast disparity in pay between the execs and the workers. Those that do the work and those that benefit from it. Start and attend meetings to discuss how others can get involved in your area. Support your neighbors without jobs while avoiding the typical "blame the poor" rhetoric – we know whose fault this is.

Boycott a company doing ill-will towards humanity. The companies killing our planet and ruining the potential for children and grandchildren.

This doesn’t have to have a name.   It’s just another movement.
